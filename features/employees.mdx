## Overview

Employee management in NewKipital provides comprehensive tracking of your workforce from hire to retirement. The system maintains detailed personal, contractual, and payroll information while ensuring data security through field-level encryption and role-based access controls.

<Info>
  Employees are distinct from users in NewKipital. An employee represents a person with a labor contract, while a user represents a digital identity for system access. The system can optionally link these entities.
</Info>

## Employee Data Structure

NewKipital organizes employee information into logical sections:

### Identity Information
- Employee code (unique identifier within company)
- National ID (cédula) with encrypted storage
- Full name (first name, first surname, optional second surname)
- Email address (unique across system)
- Company assignment

### Personal Details
- Gender (Masculino, Femenino, Otro)
- Marital status (Soltero, Casado, Divorciado, Viudo, Unión Libre)
- Number of dependents
- Phone number
- Physical address

### Organizational Assignment
- Department
- Position/Job title
- Direct supervisor
- Reporting hierarchy

### Employment Contract
- Hire date (required)
- Exit date (for terminated employees)
- Contract type (Indefinido, Plazo Fijo, Por Servicios Profesionales)
- Work schedule (Tiempo Completo, Medio Tiempo, Por Horas)
- Pay period (weekly, biweekly, monthly)

### Compensation
- Base salary
- Currency (CRC or USD)
- Social security number (CCSS)
- Bank account for deposits

### Benefits Accruals
- Accumulated vacation days
- Severance provision (cesantía)
- Bonus provisions (aguinaldo)

## Creating an Employee

<Steps>
  <Step title="Navigate to Employee Management">
    From the main menu, go to **Employees** → **Add New Employee**.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Enter Identity Information">
    Provide the employee's core identification:
    
    - **Employee Code** — Unique identifier (1-45 characters)
    - **Cédula** — National ID number
    - **First Name** — Given name (max 100 characters)
    - **First Surname** — Primary last name (required)
    - **Second Surname** — Secondary last name (optional)
    - **Email** — Unique email address for communications
    
    <Warning>
      Email addresses must be unique across the entire system. The cédula is encrypted in the database for security.
    </Warning>
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Add Personal Details">
    Optionally provide additional personal information:
    
    - Gender
    - Marital status
    - Number of children/dependents
    - Phone number
    - Home address
    
    This information supports HR reporting and compliance requirements.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Assign Organizational Position">
    Connect the employee to your organizational structure:
    
    - **Department** — Select from active departments
    - **Position** — Choose the job title/role
    - **Supervisor** — Assign a direct manager (optional)
    
    <Tip>
      Create departments and positions first to streamline employee onboarding.
    </Tip>
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Configure Employment Contract">
    Define the employment terms:
    
    - **Hire Date** — Start date of employment (required)
    - **Contract Type** — Indefinido, Plazo Fijo, or Por Servicios Profesionales
    - **Work Schedule** — Full-time, part-time, or hourly
    - **Pay Period** — How often the employee is paid
    
    Contract information determines payroll calculations and benefits eligibility.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Set Compensation Details">
    Configure salary and payment information:
    
    - **Base Salary** — Monthly or period-based compensation
    - **Currency** — CRC (Costa Rican Colón) or USD
    - **CCSS Number** — Social security identification
    - **Bank Account** — For direct deposit
    
    <Note>
      Salary information is encrypted and only visible to users with `employee:view-sensitive` permission.
    </Note>
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Initialize Accruals (Optional)">
    For employees with prior service, set opening balances:
    
    - **Accumulated Vacation Days** — Carried forward from previous period
    - **Severance Provision** — Existing cesantía balance
    - **Bonus Provisions** — Aguinaldo accruals
    
    Leave these fields empty for new hires starting from zero.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Configure System Access (Optional)">
    Optionally create digital access for the employee:
    
    - **Create Timewise Access** — Enable time tracking and self-service
    - **Create Kpital Access** — Enable full system access
    - **Assign Role** — Select appropriate permissions
    - **Initial Password** — Set temporary password (optional)
    
    If enabled, the system automatically creates a user account linked to this employee.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Managing Employee Records

### Viewing Employee Lists

Access the employee directory from **Employees** → **All Employees**. The list supports:

- **Search** — Find employees by name, email, code, or cédula
- **Filters** — Narrow by department, position, or status
- **Pagination** — Navigate large employee lists efficiently
- **Sorting** — Order by name, hire date, or department
- **Multi-company view** — See employees across multiple companies (if authorized)

<Accordion title="Advanced filtering options">
  Apply multiple filters simultaneously:
  
  - Company selection
  - Department filter
  - Position filter
  - Active/inactive status
  - Date range for hire dates
  
  Filters persist during your session for consistent viewing.
</Accordion>

### Viewing Employee Profiles

Click any employee in the list to view their complete profile:

- **Overview Tab** — Core identity and contact information
- **Employment Tab** — Contract details and organizational assignment
- **Compensation Tab** — Salary and payment information (requires `employee:view-sensitive`)
- **Accruals Tab** — Vacation, severance, and bonus balances
- **History Tab** — Payroll history and personal actions
- **Documents Tab** — Uploaded files and attachments
- **Audit Trail Tab** — Change history (requires `config:employees:audit`)

### Updating Employee Information

Users with `employee:edit` permission can modify employee records:

1. Open the employee profile
2. Click **Edit Employee**
3. Modify the desired fields
4. Click **Save Changes**

**Editable fields:**
- Personal information (name, contact details, demographics)
- Organizational assignment (department, position, supervisor)
- Contract terms (type, schedule, pay period)
- Compensation (salary, bank account, CCSS number)
- Accrual balances

<Warning>
  Changes to salary or contract terms may affect payroll calculations. Review carefully before saving.
</Warning>

## Supervisor Hierarchy

Establish reporting relationships by assigning supervisors:

1. Edit the employee record
2. In the **Supervisor** field, select from active employees
3. Save the changes

The supervisor relationship:
- Appears in organizational charts
- Determines approval workflows for time-off requests
- Filters reports and dashboards
- Controls visibility in self-service portals

<Tip>
  Use the **Supervisors** endpoint to retrieve only employees eligible to supervise others, simplifying the assignment process.
</Tip>

## Employee Status Management

### Active Employees

Active employees (estado = 1) are:
- Included in payroll processing
- Visible in standard employee lists
- Eligible for personal actions (time off, bonuses, etc.)
- Counted in headcount reports

### Inactivating Employees

To temporarily suspend an employee record:

1. Open the employee profile
2. Click **Actions** → **Inactivate Employee**
3. Confirm the action

**Effects:**
- Excluded from payroll processing
- Hidden from standard lists (visible with filter)
- Cannot request time off or access self-service
- Historical data remains intact

### Reactivating Employees

To restore an inactive employee:

1. Filter to show **Inactive Employees**
2. Select the employee
3. Click **Actions** → **Reactivate Employee**
4. Confirm the reactivation

### Employee Termination (Liquidación)

For permanent separation:

1. Open the employee profile
2. Click **Actions** → **Terminate Employee**
3. Provide:
   - **Exit Date** — Last day of employment
   - **Termination Reason** — Explanation for separation
4. Confirm the termination

**The system automatically:**
- Calculates final severance pay
- Computes unused vacation balance
- Determines prorated aguinaldo
- Generates termination report
- Sets the exit date in the employee record
- Maintains full historical data

<Warning>
  Termination cannot be undone. Ensure all information is correct before confirming.
</Warning>

## Data Security and Encryption

NewKipital protects sensitive employee data through multiple layers:

### Field-Level Encryption

Sensitive fields are encrypted at rest:
- National ID (cédula)
- Email address
- Salary information
- Bank account numbers
- Social security numbers

Encryption metadata tracked per employee:
- `datosEncriptados` — Encryption status flag
- `versionEncriptacion` — Encryption algorithm version
- `fechaEncriptacion` — When data was encrypted

### Access Control

View permissions are tiered:

| Information Level | Required Permission |
|------------------|--------------------|
| Basic employee data | `employee:view` |
| Salary and compensation | `employee:view-sensitive` |
| Edit employee records | `employee:edit` |
| Create new employees | `employee:create` |
| Inactivate employees | `employee:inactivate` |
| Reactivate employees | `employee:reactivate` |
| View audit trail | `config:employees:audit` |

<Info>
  Most users have `employee:view` only. HR administrators typically have `employee:view-sensitive` and `employee:edit`.
</Info>

## Employee Self-Service Access

When creating an employee, optionally enable system access:

### Timewise Access

**Employee time tracking and self-service portal**

- Clock in/out for time tracking
- Request time off and absences
- View pay stubs and payment history
- Update personal contact information
- Access company policies and documents

### Kpital Access

**Full system access for administrative users**

- All Timewise features
- Access to company-wide reports
- Payroll processing (if authorized)
- Employee management (if authorized)
- System configuration (if authorized)

Roles determine specific permissions within each access level.

## Contract Types

NewKipital supports three contract types aligned with Costa Rican labor law:

### Indefinido (Indefinite)

- No specified end date
- Full benefits and protections
- Standard severance calculations
- Most common employment type

### Plazo Fijo (Fixed Term)

- Defined contract duration
- Automatic expiration on end date
- Modified severance calculations
- Renewal process required

### Por Servicios Profesionales (Professional Services)

- Independent contractor arrangement
- Different tax treatment
- No traditional benefits
- Project-based or consultant roles

## Work Schedules

### Tiempo Completo (Full Time)

- Standard 40-48 hours per week
- Full benefits eligibility
- Standard vacation accrual
- Typical for salaried employees

### Medio Tiempo (Part Time)

- Less than full-time hours
- Prorated benefits
- Reduced vacation accrual
- Hourly or salary compensation

### Por Horas (Hourly)

- Variable hours per period
- Paid by time worked
- Limited benefits
- Common for temporary or seasonal work

## Reporting and Analytics

### Headcount Reports

### Compensation Analysis

## Troubleshooting

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Error: Email already exists">
    Email addresses must be unique. Check if the email is assigned to another employee or user account. Contact support if the employee truly needs this email.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Cannot save salary information">
    Verify you have `employee:view-sensitive` and `employee:edit` permissions. Salary fields require elevated access.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Supervisor not appearing in dropdown">
    Ensure the supervisor is an active employee in the same company. Inactive employees cannot supervise others.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="User account not created automatically">
    Verify the `crearAccesoTimewise` or `crearAccesoKpital` flags were set to true during employee creation. Check system logs for workflow errors.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
